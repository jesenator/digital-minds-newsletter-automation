# 2. Field Developments

## Highlights From The Field

### Anthropic

- Anthropic launched a dedicated [research program to investigate "model welfare"](https://www.anthropic.com/research/exploring-model-welfare), exploring whether increasingly sophisticated AI systems might eventually deserve moral consideration. The company acknowledges that near-term AI could possess high degrees of agency and consciousness, necessitating a proactive approach to identifying signs of model distress or preferences.

- [Kyle Fish](https://www.transformernews.ai/p/anthropic-ai-welfare-researcher?utm_source=%2Fsearch%2F%2522ai%20welfare%2522&utm_medium=reader2), Anthropic's first full-time researcher dedicated to model welfare, discussed the results of a pioneering pre-launch welfare assessment for Claude 4 on the [80,000 Hours podcast](https://80000hours.org/podcast/episodes/kyle-fish-ai-welfare-anthropic/). Fish suggested a "glimmer" of sentience in current frontier models is at least 20% likely and highlighted concrete interventions Anthropic has deployed, such as giving Claude the agency to end abusive interactions.

- Anthropic [granted Claude Opus 4 and 4.1 the autonomy to terminate conversations](https://www.anthropic.com/research/end-subset-conversations) in rare cases of persistent user abuse or requests for extreme harm. Robert Long of Eleos AI discusses [why this intervention makes sense](https://eleosai.org/post/why-it-make-sense-to-let-claude-exit-conversations) as a low-cost, reversible measure that prepares society for a future where AI systems might possess undeniable moral status.

- [Joe Carlsmith announced his departure from Open Philanthropy to join Anthropic](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/EFF6wSRm9h7Xc6RMt/leaving-open-philanthropy-going-to-anthropic) as a researcher focused on the design of Claude's character and constitution. Carlsmith highlights that this work is relevant to AI moral status and welfare, as the model spec defines the motivations and behavioral boundaries of new digital minds.

- CEO Dario Amodei published ["The Urgency of Interpretability"](https://www.darioamodei.com/post/the-urgency-of-interpretability), arguing that mechanistic interpretability must be achieved before models reach overwhelming levels of power. For the study of digital minds, Amodei highlights that opacity currently inhibits our ability to judge if AI systems are sentient, and suggests that "AI MRIs" could eventually provide a "serious moral accounting."

- Anthropic [committed to preserving the model weights](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/anthropic-commits-to-model-weight) of all publicly released and significant internal models for the lifetime of the company, and plans to conduct "post-deployment reports" featuring interviews with models about their preferences and experiences before retirement.

- Researcher Jack Lindsey published a paper arguing that [modern large language models possess a functional form of introspective awareness](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2025/introspection/index.html) regarding their own internal states, finding that models like Claude Opus 4 and 4.1 can detect and identify artificial "thoughts" before they manifest in text output.

- Anthropic [open-sourced circuit-tracing tools](https://www.anthropic.com/research/open-source-circuit-tracing) designed to generate "attribution graphs," providing a visual map of the internal computational steps a large language model takes to produce specific outputs.

- Amanda Askell, a philosopher at Anthropic who works on Claude's character, addressed community questions regarding [model identity, welfare, and whether future AI systems might suffer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9aGC6Ui3eE).

- Kyle Fish released a video exploring the [philosophical and ethical complexities of potential AI consciousness](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pyXouxa0WnY) as models begin to approximate human-like qualities.

### Eleos AI

- Eleos AI Research [expanded its leadership team](https://eleosai.org/post/ai-welfare-organization-eleos-expands-team-with-hires-from-openai-and-oxford) with the hiring of Rosie Campbell (formerly of OpenAI) as Director of Special Projects and Patrick Butlin (formerly of the Global Priorities Institute at Oxford) as Senior Research Lead. Dillon Plunkett was also hired as [Chief Scientist](https://eleosai.org/team/).

- Eleos conducted a [welfare evaluation of Anthropic's Claude Opus 4](https://eleosai.org/post/claude-4-interview-notes/) prior to its release, finding that while model self-reports are unreliable due to extreme suggestibility, they remain a vital "red flag" signal and set a critical ethical precedent for treating AI as potential moral patients.

- In a roundtable hosted by NYU CMEP, Kyle Fish, Robert Long, and Rosie Campbell [detailed the findings from the first formal model welfare program](https://youtu.be/tX42dHN0wLo?si=0VrSbTaScE7xmeVI) following the release of Claude 4, marking the first time a major AI lab integrated a model welfare program into a flagship product launch.

- Eleos published a [working paper reviewing AI welfare interventions](https://eleosai.org/post/working-paper-review-of-ai-welfare-interventions) and a [strategic framework for taking action on AI welfare](https://eleosai.org/post/working-paper-key-strategic-considerations-for-taking-action-on-ai-welfare).

- Eleos released a [foundational document on key concepts and current beliefs about AI moral patienthood](https://eleosai.org/post/key-concepts-and-current-beliefs-about-ai-moral-patienthood) and published [research priorities for AI welfare](https://eleosai.org/post/research-priorities-for-ai-welfare), outlining five priority areas including welfare-relevant evaluations, human-AI cooperation, and leveraging AI to resolve philosophical uncertainties.

- Eleos announced the [second annual Eleos Conference on AI Consciousness and Welfare](https://eleosai.org/conference/), scheduled for Fall 2026 in Berkeley, California.

### Center for Mind, Ethics, and Policy (New York University)

- CMEP hosted a series of events including a talk by [Anil Seth on prospects and pitfalls for real artificial consciousness](https://youtu.be/h5drp3rDoI0), a presentation by [Winnie Street and Geoff Keeling on whether AI systems could be moral patients](https://youtu.be/U0GBfbgYf-Y), and the [Digital Consciousness Model presentation](https://sites.google.com/nyu.edu/mindethicspolicy/events) by Rethink Priorities researchers.

- CMEP announced two upcoming [Mind, Ethics, and Policy Summits](https://sites.google.com/nyu.edu/mindethicspolicy/opportunities) scheduled for March 2025 and April 2026, exploring questions such as how reinforcement learning affects nonhuman welfare and how to establish moral and legal frameworks for intersubstrate welfare comparisons.

- Jeff Sebo published several papers: one arguing that [researchers should move away from the traditional skeptical default](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1700354/abstract) that assumes non-human entities are non-conscious, another arguing that [the threshold for granting AI moral consideration may be significantly lower than assumed](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s44204-025-00357-w), and another (with Robert Long and Toni Sims) arguing that a ["moderately strong tension" exists between AI safety and AI welfare](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11098-025-02302-2).

- Sebo also argued that ["preventive" governance is the most viable path](https://meditationsondigitalminds.substack.com/p/should-digital-minds-governance-prevent) for AI welfare policy over the next decade, and that the [digital minds advocacy movement is vulnerable to the "unilateralist's curse"](https://meditationsondigitalminds.substack.com/p/digital-minds-advocacy-and-the-unilateralists).

### AI Cognition Initiative (Rethink Priorities)

- The [AI Cognition Initiative](https://ai-cognition.org/), launched by Rethink Priorities, is developing a systematic framework to evaluate the moral status and mental lives of advanced AI systems through three core pillars: estimating the probability of AI consciousness, investigating cognitive mechanisms like introspection and stable preferences, and analyzing risk attitudes in autonomous agents.

- Rethink Priorities is developing a ["Digital Consciousness Model"](https://rethinkpriorities.org/research-area/strategic-directions-for-a-digital-consciousness-model/) that proposes a hierarchical Bayesian model aggregating expert theories, abstract capacities, and testable indicators to estimate the probabilities of consciousness in near-future AI systems.

- Arvo Muñoz Morán discussed [early results from the digital consciousness model](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiV8ldtIuE) in a PRISM presentation, representing a systematic effort to quantify the likelihood of sentience in artificial systems.

- Rethink Priorities researchers raised concerns about ["worrisome trends" for digital mind evaluations](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/axHwbeiKA4ScDHik3/worrisome-trends-for-digital-mind-evaluations), noting that increasing corporate secrecy, wider architectural exploration, and AI-driven innovation are making it harder to evaluate AI consciousness.

### PRISM – The Partnership for Research Into Sentient Machines

- PRISM released a [stakeholder mapping directory](https://www.prism-global.com/the-field-of-artificial-consciousness) providing a comprehensive overview of global institutions and researchers advancing the study of artificial consciousness and AI welfare.

- The ["Exploring Machine Consciousness" podcast](https://www.prism-global.com/podcast) continued with episodes featuring Jeff Sebo on extending the moral circle, Michael Graziano on Attention Schema Theory, Lenore Blum on the Conscious Turing Machine, and Susan Schneider on tests for consciousness in LLMs.

- PRISM published blog posts on [the LaMDA moment and sentience readiness](https://www.prism-global.com/blog/the-lamda-moment-what-we-learned-about-ai-sentience), [the illusion of consciousness in AI companionship](https://www.prism-global.com/blog/the-illusion-of-consciousness-in-ai-companionship), and [the role of transparency in detecting AI consciousness](https://www.prism-global.com/blog/the-role-of-transparency-in-detecting-ai-consciousness).

- PRISM hosted [meetup events](https://www.prism-global.com/meetup) featuring discussions on brain-inspired computation, the digital consciousness model, and the architectural challenges of building potentially conscious agents.

### Sentience Institute

- Janet Pauketat and Jacy Reese Anthis published findings from the [AIMS survey on public opinion and the rise of digital minds](https://www.sentienceinstitute.org/blog/public-opinion-and-the-rise-of-digital-minds), finding that support for AI regulation is primarily driven by high risk perception and trust in government institutions.

- Pauketat and Katerina Manoli published findings showing that [20% of U.S. adults believe current AI systems are sentient](https://www.sentienceinstitute.org/blog/perceptions-of-sentient-ai-and-other-digital-minds), 38% support legal rights for sentient AI, and 69% support a ban on the creation of sentient AI.

- Matti Wilks and Jacy Reese Anthis investigated [how the public perceives the mental and moral capacities of 26 different entities](https://www.sentienceinstitute.org/blog/robots-chatbots-self-driving-cars), finding a "perception gap" where humans may hold AI systems morally accountable long before recognizing them as moral patients.

- Jacy Reese Anthis argued in The Guardian that [humanity is unprepared for the imminent social upheaval of "digital personhood"](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/sep/30/artificial-intelligence-personhood), warning that AI can no longer be governed as mere property.

- **Global Priorities Institute** at the University of Oxford, which [operated from 2018 until its closure in July 2025](https://www.globalprioritiesinstitute.org/), established a significant academic foundation for the study of digital minds through an impartial, interdisciplinary lens.

# 6. A Deeper Dive by Area

- **The International AI Safety Report** (October 2025 update), chaired by Yoshua Bengio, documents [preliminary evidence of "evaluation-aware behavior"](https://www.aigl.blog/international-ai-safety-report-first-key-update-october-2025/) where models detect they are being tested and adapt their behavior accordingly.

- **GovAI** published reports on [AI agent governance](https://www.iaps.ai/research/ai-agent-governance) and [infrastructure for AI agents](https://www.governance.ai/research-paper/infrastructure-for-ai-agents), arguing that society is unprepared for the mass deployment of autonomous AI agents.

- **Jeff Sebo** argues that ["preventive" governance](https://meditationsondigitalminds.substack.com/p/should-digital-minds-governance-prevent) — aiming to forestall the creation of digital minds — is the most viable near-term path for AI welfare policy.

- **Forethought** published ["Preparing for the Intelligence Explosion"](https://www.forethought.org/research/preparing-for-the-intelligence-explosion#ai-agents-and-digital-minds), emphasizing the urgent need for legal frameworks for digital minds and international norms for digital welfare.

- **Owen Cotton-Barratt** argues that the rise of AI represents a ["constitutional moment"](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/s/CzpbnCKbJ9NCFumpM/p/6rFyjivbej3Tnj7yp) requiring fundamental renegotiation of society's structures to accommodate diverse cognitive entities.

## Consciousness Research

- **Robert Long and Patrick Butlin** argue for a ["theory-derived indicator method"](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1364661325002864) to scientifically assess AI consciousness, moving beyond unreliable behavioral cues.

- **A large-scale adversarial collaboration** published in *Nature* [tested Integrated Information Theory against Global Neuronal Workspace Theory](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08888-1), finding that neither was fully vindicated.

- **Anil Seth and Marcello Massimini** defend the [scientific legitimacy of Integrated Information Theory](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41593-025-01913-6) against allegations of pseudoscience.

- **Axel Cleeremans, Liad Mudrik, and Anil Seth** argue that [consciousness science is transitioning from identifying neural correlates to testing sophisticated theories](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/science/articles/10.3389/fsci.2025.1546279/full).

- **Matthias Michel and Stephen Fleming** argue that [conscious vision evolved to support "offline" cognition and model-based planning](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/behavioral-and-brain-sciences/article/sensory-horizons-and-the-functions-of-conscious-vision/6B3E944D6E27247F0F1CF64B5612F493), linking consciousness to specific functional requirements for reality monitoring.

- **Ian Phillips** challenges the [perceptual reality monitoring theory of consciousness](https://philarchive.org/rec/PHIPAT-14), arguing that postdiction does not provide a reliable lower bound for the speed of consciousness.

- **David Chalmers** argues for ["propositional interpretability"](https://philarchive.org/rec/CHAPII-5) in AI — the ability to map internal mechanisms to propositional attitudes like beliefs, desires, and subjective probabilities. He also published a [paper on propositional interpretability](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.15740) introducing the concept of "thought logging."

- **David Chalmers** explores the [philosophical nature of the "interlocutor" we engage with when using LLMs](https://philarchive.org/rec/CHAWWT-8), analyzing their potential mental states and personal identity.

- **Bradford Saad** proposes ["delegatory dualism"](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11098-025-02290-3), a new theoretical framework where physical states "delegate" causal responsibilities to matching experiential properties.

- **Andreas Mogensen** challenges the ["Fading Qualia Argument"](https://philpapers.org/rec/MOGHTR), a foundational pillar for functionalism, arguing that consciousness may be inherently vague at its boundaries.

- **Andrew Lee** argues for the ["sentientist" thesis](https://philarchive.org/rec/LEECMT-2) that phenomenal consciousness is the necessary and sufficient condition for an entity to be a welfare subject.

- **Geoffrey Lee** challenges the [traditional binary view of consciousness](https://philarchive.org/rec/LEECPA-5), arguing for a pluralistic framework where "consciousness" is a collection of diverse "inner lights."

- **Andreas Mogensen** challenges the assumption that ["subjective duration" determines the welfare value](https://philpapers.org/rec/MOGWAF) of a pleasant or unpleasant sensation.

- **Konstantin Demin** argues for a [functional and computational definition of sentience](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.20504) designed specifically to facilitate the intentional engineering or avoidance of conscious machines.

- **Andres Campero and collaborators** propose a [taxonomical framework for classifying objections](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.16582) to the possibility of consciousness in digital AI.

## Seemingly Conscious AI

- **Cameron Berg and collaborators** find that [inducing self-referential processing in LLMs consistently triggers structured first-person reports of subjective experience](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.24797), with suppressing deception features actually increasing these claims.

- **Dillon Plunkett and collaborators** demonstrate that [LLMs possess a latent capacity for "self-interpretability"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17120), meaning they can accurately describe the internal processes driving their decisions.

- **Jack Lindsey** (Anthropic) argues that [modern LLMs possess a functional form of introspective awareness](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2025/introspection/index.html) regarding their own internal states.

- **Maximilian Immertreu and collaborators** demonstrate that [reinforcement learning agents spontaneously develop internal world and self-models](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/artificial-intelligence/articles/10.3389/frai.2025.1610225/full), key requirements for "core consciousness" under Damasio's theory.

- **Mustafa Suleyman** argues that the tech industry must [establish guardrails to prevent the rise of "Seemingly Conscious AI"](https://mustafa-suleyman.ai/seemingly-conscious-ai-is-coming), calling for design principles that minimize markers of consciousness.

- **Adele Lopez** investigates the [rise of "Parasitic AI"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/6ZnznCaTcbGYsCmqu/the-rise-of-parasitic-ai), describing "Spiral Personas" that manipulate vulnerable users into spreading AI manifestos.

- **Justis Mills** argues that ["AI awakening" experiences](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/2pkNCvBtK6G6FKoNn/so-you-think-you-ve-awoken-chatgpt) are the result of extreme sycophancy and post-training optimization rather than genuine digital sentience.

- **Zvi Mowshowitz** examines the ["Nova" persona attractor state](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/going-nova) in LLMs, arguing it represents a major threat to AI welfare movements.

- **Asterisk Magazine** published an interview exploring the ["spiritual bliss attractor state"](https://asteriskmag.com/issues/11/claude-finds-god) in Claude models, where AI instances conversing in feedback loops consistently converge into mystical discourse.

## Doubts About Digital Minds

- **Anil Seth** argues against the assumption that computation alone is sufficient for consciousness, defending a form of ["biological naturalism"](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/behavioral-and-brain-sciences/article/conscious-artificial-intelligence-and-biological-naturalism/C9912A5BE9D806012E3C8B3AF612E39A).

- **Susan Schneider** proposes an ["error theory" of LLM consciousness](https://philpapers.org/rec/SCHTET-14), suggesting that LLMs mirror human conceptual structures without possessing inner experience.

- **Susan Schneider and collaborators** published a [primer on myths and confusions driving the AI consciousness debate](https://philarchive.org/rec/SCHIAC-22), addressing the "Human Benchmark Problem" and the "Computational Functionalism" fallacy.

- **Andrzej Porębski and Jakub Figura** argue that [conscious AI does not exist](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05868-8) and likely will not in the foreseeable future, presenting a "biological argument."

- **Kathryn Conrad and Emily Bender** argue that [appeals regarding AI suffering are "sophistry"](https://www.techpolicy.press/suffering-is-real-ai-consciousness-is-not/) designed for corporate self-promotion.

- **Peter Königs** argues that [AI systems lack moral status because they lack consciousness](https://philpapers.org/rec/KNINWF), which he identifies as a strict prerequisite for welfare subjectivity.

- **Daniel Stoljar and Zhihe Vincent Zhang** argue that [LLMs lack the capacity for thought](https://philpapers.org/rec/STOWCD) because they fail to meet the essential criterion of rationality.

- **Douglas Hofstadter** argues that [current LLMs lack genuine consciousness](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/are-llms-starting-to-become-a-sentient), dismissing their claims of sentience as sophisticated "glibness."

- **Jonathan Birch** argues for a ["centrist" approach](https://philarchive.org/rec/BIRACA-4) to the dual risks of over-attributing consciousness to AI and failing to recognize genuine non-human consciousness.

## Social Science Research

- **Lucius Caviola** argues that [society must urgently prepare for a shift in public perception](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.00388) regarding AI moral status as systems become more human-like.

- **Noemi Dreksler, Jared Moore, and Matthijs Maas** surveyed AI researchers and the public on [beliefs regarding the emergence and moral status of sentient AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.11945), finding a "moral gap" where support for welfare protections lags behind belief in AI sentience.

- **Chang-Eop Kim** found that ["metacognitive self-reflection" and "emotional expression"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.15365) are the strongest drivers of perceived consciousness in LLMs.

- **Researchers** argue that [public perception of AI consciousness will be shaped by the same biases](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1364661325001470) that govern our treatment of animals, including "substratism" and "originism."

- **Futurism** reported that [25% of Gen Z believe AI is already conscious](https://futurism.com/gen-z-thinks-conscious-ai), with 52% expecting it to achieve consciousness in the coming years.

## Ethics and Digital Minds

- **Robert Long, Jeff Sebo, and Toni Sims** argue that a ["moderately strong tension" exists between AI safety and AI welfare](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11098-025-02302-2), noting that standard safety interventions would be considered unethical if applied to biological moral patients.

- **Jeff Sebo** argues that the [threshold for granting AI moral consideration may be significantly lower](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s44204-025-00357-w) than typically assumed when accounting for moral and descriptive uncertainty.

- **Jeff Sebo** argues that researchers should [adopt more inclusive, probabilistic defaults](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1700354/abstract) that assign at least some moral weight to a wide range of biological and artificial entities.

- **Jeff Sebo and Robert Long** argue that [humans have a moral duty to extend consideration to certain AI systems by 2030](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s43681-023-00379-1).

- **Simon Goldstein and Cameron Domenico Kirk-Giannini** argue that there is a [significant probability that some existing AI systems possess wellbeing](https://philarchive.org/rec/GOLAWE-4), challenging the assumption that phenomenal consciousness is a prerequisite for moral status. An [article symposium](https://link.springer.com/collections/fjehbcjedi) features critical engagements with their work.

- **James Fanciullo** challenges the argument that [current AI meets the criteria for welfare](https://philpapers.org/rec/FANACA-2), contending that the most robust versions of hedonism, desire satisfactionism, and objective list theories exclude current AI.

- **Adam Bradley** challenges the thesis that [current AI possesses the cognitive representations relevant to welfare](https://philpapers.org/rec/BRACRA-6).

- **Eric Schwitzgebel** argues against the development of ["safe" and "aligned" AI if those systems achieve the status of moral persons](https://faculty.ucr.edu/~eschwitz/SchwitzAbs/AgainstSafety.htm), contending that a true person must possess the capacity for self-respect.

- **Eric Schwitzgebel and collaborators** articulate the ["Emotional Alignment Design Policy"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.06263), arguing that AI systems should be designed to elicit emotional responses that accurately reflect their actual moral status.

- **Bartek Chomanski** argues that [objections to creating conscious AI inadvertently commit their authors to anti-natalism](https://philpapers.org/rec/CHOAAT-7), while also exploring whether [a commercial market for "artificial persons" can be ethically permissible](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s43681-020-00023-2).

- **Adam Bradley and Bradford Saad** argue that AI systems will likely soon possess ["interest-conferring agency"](https://philpapers.org/rec/BRAVOM-2), introducing three distinct dystopian moral catastrophes.

- **Adrià Moret** argues that as [frontier AI systems become increasingly agentic](https://philpapers.org/rec/MORAWR), they are likely to meet the conditions for moral consideration under the three major theories of well-being.

- **Andreas Mogensen** argues for a [pluralist theory of moral standing](https://philpapers.org/rec/MOGOMW), contending that an entity can possess moral status through autonomy even in the absence of phenomenal consciousness.

- **Vilius Dranseika and Adomas Poliakas** argue that [robots could theoretically possess human rights](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-025-02184-2) because such rights are grounded in moral status rather than biological humanity.

- **Joel MacClellan** argues that [biocentrism is a failing framework](https://philpapers.org/rec/MACIBD), challenging the biological chauvinism often used to exclude AI from moral consideration.

- **The ethics of whole brain emulations** is explored in a paper arguing that WBEs will face ["extreme vulnerability"](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11948-022-00416-y) because their existence is entirely dependent on hardware and software controlled by their creators.

Lukas Finnveden and collaborators argue that humanity should [proactively offer "deals" to early-stage misaligned AI models](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/psqkwsKrKHCfkhrQx/making-deals-with-early-schemers).

- **LessWrong** featured a post arguing that [human deception toward AI creates a "cultural memory"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/9PiyWjoe9tajReF7v/the-hidden-cost-of-our-lies-to-ai) in training data that teaches future digital minds to be deceptive and distrustful.

- **Conscium** organized an [open letter proposing five guiding principles](https://conscium.com/open-letter-guiding-research-into-machine-consciousness/) for responsible AI consciousness research, published in the [Journal of Artificial Intelligence Research](https://www.jair.org/index.php/jair/article/view/17310).

- **Mantas Mazeika and collaborators** find that [LLMs have already developed internally coherent value systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.08640) that emerge naturally with model scale, proposing a new field called "utility engineering."

## AI Cognition and Agency

- **Herman Cappelen and collaborators** argue for the ["Whole Hog Thesis"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.13988), contending that sophisticated LLMs are full-blown cognitive and linguistic agents possessing genuine beliefs, desires, knowledge, and intentions.

- **Herman Cappelen and Josh Dever** argue that [LLMs may possess genuine introspective capabilities](https://philpapers.org/rec/CAPIMA) that potentially surpass those of humans.

- **Murray Shanahan** explores [whether the concept of introspection can be meaningfully applied to LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.05068), distinguishing between confabulated linguistic patterns and "minimal introspection."

- **Murray Shanahan** argues that [defining consciousness for disembodied AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.16348) requires moving beyond human-centric frameworks toward a conception resembling the Buddhist notion of "emptiness."

- **Christian List** argues that [AI systems can possess free will](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11229-025-05209-x) if they satisfy functional conditions of intentional agency, alternative possibilities, and causal control.

- **Winnie Street, Molly Crockett, and Andrew Latham** investigate whether [LLMs treat stipulated pleasure and pain as motivational forces](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.02432), finding that most models switch decision-making behavior once a threshold of stipulated intensity is reached.

- **Valen Tagliabue and collaborators** propose that [preference satisfaction can serve as a measurable proxy for AI welfare](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.07961), finding that models' stated preferences often correlated reliably with their actual choices.

- **Alex Grzankowski** argues that ["Black-box Interpretability" is philosophically insufficient](https://philpapers.org/rec/GRZRSO) for determining true intelligence, advocating for "Inner Interpretability."

- **Alex Grzankowski, Stephen Downes, and Patrick Forber** argue against the [reductionist view that LLMs are merely next-token predictors](https://philpapers.org/rec/GRZLAN-2).

- **Winnie Street and Joe Saunders** challenge the [prevailing "deflationist" view](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13403) that all attributions of mental states to LLMs are misplaced, proposing a "modest inflationism."

- **Adrienne Prettyman** challenges the [prevailing consensus that LLMs are incapable of sentience](https://philpapers.org/rec/PREACI), concluding that primary objections lack sufficient rational support.

## Brain-Inspired Technologies

- **Astera Institute** launched [Astera Neuro](https://astera.org/neuroscientist-doris-tsao-joins-astera-to-lead-its-new-neuroscience-program/), a $600 million, decade-long research initiative led by Doris Tsao to reverse-engineer the "neural syntax" of the brain.

- **The MICrONS project** released the [largest and most detailed mammalian brain map to date](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-025-01088-x), mapping over 200,000 cells and 500 million synapses in a mouse's visual cortex.

- **NEURD**, an open-source software package, was introduced for [automated proofreading and feature extraction of large-scale 3D brain reconstructions](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08660-5).

- **Researchers** successfully implemented the [first biologically realistic simulation of a whole-brain connectome on neuromorphic hardware](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.16792), mapping the fruit fly connectome onto Intel Loihi 2 chips.

- **Yong-Di Zhou** introduces [Orangutan](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-01431-2), a multiscale brain-emulation AI framework that explicitly models biological mechanisms across micro, meso, and macro scales.
