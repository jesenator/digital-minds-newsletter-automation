# 1\. Highlights

In 2025, the idea of digital minds shifted from a niche research topic to one taken seriously by a growing number of researchers, AI developers, and philanthropic funders. Questions about real or perceived AI consciousness and moral status appeared regularly in tech reporting, academic discussions, and public discourse.

## Anthropic's early steps on model welfare

Following their support for the 2024 report "[Taking AI Welfare Seriously](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.00986)", Anthropic expanded its [model welfare efforts](https://www.anthropic.com/research/exploring-model-welfare) in 2025 and [hired](https://www.transformernews.ai/p/anthropic-ai-welfare-researcher?utm_source=%2Fsearch%2F%2522ai%2520welfare%2522&utm_medium=reader2) Kyle Fish as an AI welfare researcher. Fish discussed the topic and his work in an 80,000 Hours [interview](https://80000hours.org/podcast/episodes/kyle-fish-ai-welfare-anthropic/). Anthropic leadership is taking the issue of AI welfare seriously. CEO Dario Amodei [drew attention](https://www.darioamodei.com/post/the-urgency-of-interpretability#:~:text=There%20are%20other,Brief%20History%20of) to the relevance of model interpretability to model welfare and [mentioned model exit rights](https://x.com/rgblong/status/1900332240338641249?s=20) at the council on foreign relations. 

Several of the year's most notable developments came from Anthropic: they facilitated an [external model welfare assessment](https://eleosai.org/post/claude-4-interview-notes/) conducted by Eleos AI Research, included references to welfare considerations in model [system](https://www-cdn.anthropic.com/6be99a52cb68eb70eb9572b4cafad13df32ed995.pdf) [cards](https://assets.anthropic.com/m/12f214efcc2f457a/original/Claude-Sonnet-4-5-System-Card.pdf#page116), ran a related fellowship program, introduced a ["bail button"](https://www.anthropic.com/research/end-subset-conversations) for distressed behavior, and made internal commitments around keeping promises and discretionary compute. In addition to hiring Fish, Anthropic also [hired a philosopher--Joe Carlsmith](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/EFF6wSRm9h7Xc6RMt/leaving-open-philanthropy-going-to-anthropic)--who has worked on [AI moral patiency](https://joecarlsmith.com/2025/05/21/the-stakes-of-ai-moral-status/).

## The field is growing

In the **non-profit space** , [Eleos AI Research](https://eleosai.org/) expanded its work and organized the [Conference on AI Consciousness and Welfare](https://eleosai.org/conference/), while two new non-profits, [PRISM](https://www.prism-global.com/) and [CIMC](https://cimc.ai/), also launched. AI for Animals rebranded to [Sentient Futures](https://www.sentientfutures.ai/), with a broader remit including digital minds, and [Rethink Priorities](https://rethinkpriorities.org/research-area/strategic-directions-for-a-digital-consciousness-model/) refined their digital consciousness model. 

**Academic institutions** undertook novel research (see below) and organized important events, including workshops run by the [NYU Center for Mind, Ethics, and Policy](https://sites.google.com/nyu.edu/mindethicspolicy/opportunities), the [London School of Economics](https://philevents.org/event/show/134626), and the [University of Hong Kong](https://philevents.org/event/show/126442).

In the **private sector** , Anthropic has been leading the way (see section above), but others have also been making strides. Google researchers organized an AI consciousness conference three years after firing Blake Lemoine. AE Studio expanded its research into [subjective experiences in LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.24797). And Conscium launched an [open letter](https://conscium.com/open-letter-guiding-research-into-machine-consciousness/) encouraging a responsible approach to AI consciousness.

**Philanthropic actors** have also played a key role this year. The [Digital Sentience Consortium](https://www.longview.org/digital-sentience-consortium/), coordinated by Longview Philanthropy, issued the first large-scale funding call specifically for research, field-building, and applied work on AI consciousness, sentience, and moral status.

## Early signs of public discourse

Media coverage of AI consciousness, seemingly conscious behavior, and phenomena such as ["AI psychosis"](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/oct/28/ai-psychosis-chatgpt-openai-sam-altman) increased noticeably. Much of the debate focused on [whether emotionally compelling AI behavior poses risks](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c74933vzx2yo), often assuming consciousness is unlikely. High-profile comments, such as those by [Mustafa Suleyman](https://mustafa-suleyman.ai/seemingly-conscious-ai-is-coming), and [widespread user reports](https://futurism.com/gen-z-thinks-conscious-ai) added to the confusion, prompting a group of researchers (including us) to create the [WhenAISeemsConscious.org](http://whenaiseemsconscious.org) guide. In addition, major outlets such as the [BBC](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c0k3700zljjo), [CNBC](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=otAWu-bLv0Q&t=1808s), [The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/04/24/technology/ai-welfare-anthropic-claude.html), and [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2025/feb/03/ai-systems-could-be-caused-to-suffer-if-consciousness-achieved-says-research) published pieces on the possibility of AI consciousness.

## Research advances

Patrick Butlin and collaborators published a [theory-derived indicator method for assessing AI systems for consciousness](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1364661325002864), which is an updated version of the [2023 report](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2308.08708). Empirical work by Anthropic researcher Jack Lindsey explored [the introspective capacities of LLMs](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2025/introspection/index.html), as did [work by Dillon Plunkett and collaborators](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17120). David Chalmers released papers on [interpretability](https://philarchive.org/rec/CHAPII-5) and [what we talk to when we talk to LLMs](https://philarchive.org/rec/CHAWWT-8). In our own research, we conducted an [expert forecasting survey](https://digitalminds.report/forecasting-2025/) on digital minds, finding that most assign at least a 4.5% probability to conscious AI existing in 2025 and at least a 50% probability to conscious AI arriving by 2050.

# 4\. Selected Reading, Watching, & Listening

## Books

In 2025, the following book drafts were posted, and the following books were published or announced:

  * **Jeff Sebo** released _[The Moral Circle: Who Matters, What Matters, and Why](https://wwnorton.com/books/9781324064817)_ , arguing to expand moral consideration to include non-human animals and artificial systems.

  * **Kristina Šekrst** published _[The Illusion Engine: The Quest for Machine Consciousness](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-032-05562-0)_ , which is a textbook on artificial minds that interweaves philosophy and engineering.

  * **Leonard Dung 's** _[Saving Artificial Minds: Understanding and Preventing AI Suffering](https://www.routledge.com/Saving-Artificial-Minds-Understanding-and-Preventing-AI-Suffering/Dung/p/book/9781041144663)_ explores why the prevention of AI suffering should be a global priority.

  * **Nathan Rourke** in _[Mind Crime: The Moral Frontier of Artificial Intelligence](https://www.amazon.com/Mind-Crime-Frontier-Artificial-Intelligence/dp/B0F3XN2NH8) _examines whether we may be headed for a moral catastrophe in which digital minds are mistreated on a vast scale.

  * **Soenke Ziesche and Roman Yampolskiy** released _[Considerations on the AI Endgame](https://www.routledge.com/Considerations-on-the-AI-Endgame-Ethics-Risks-and-Computational-Frameworks/Ziesche-Yampolskiy/p/book/9781032933832)_. It covers AI welfare science, value alignment, identity, and proposals for universal AI ethics.

  * **Eric Schwitzgebel** released a draft of _[AI and Consciousness](https://open.substack.com/pub/eschwitz/p/new-book-in-draft-ai-and-consciousness?r=2b98v8&selection=bf61ca02-d295-4826-bfc5-25515d28cb1e&utm_campaign=post-share-selection&utm_medium=web&aspectRatio=instagram&bgColor=%232EE240&textColor=%23ffffff)_. It's a skeptical overview of the literature on AI consciousness.

  * **Geoff Keeling and Winnie Street** announced a forthcoming book called _[Emerging Questions on](https://geoffkeeling.github.io/#:~:text=Book%20on%20AI%20welfare%20forthcoming%20with%20Cambridge%20University%20Press%2C%20co%2Dauthored%20with%20Winnie%20Street.%20You%20can%20hear%20us%20talk%20about%20it%20here)**[](https://geoffkeeling.github.io/#:~:text=Book%20on%20AI%20welfare%20forthcoming%20with%20Cambridge%20University%20Press%2C%20co%2Dauthored%20with%20Winnie%20Street.%20You%20can%20hear%20us%20talk%20about%20it%20here)**[AI Welfare](https://geoffkeeling.github.io/#:~:text=Book%20on%20AI%20welfare%20forthcoming%20with%20Cambridge%20University%20Press%2C%20co%2Dauthored%20with%20Winnie%20Street.%20You%20can%20hear%20us%20talk%20about%20it%20here)_ with Cambridge University Press.

## Ethics and digital minds

  * **Eleos AI** [outlines five research priorities for AI welfare](https://eleosai.org/post/research-priorities-for-ai-welfare/): developing concrete interventions, establishing human-AI cooperation frameworks, leveraging AI progress to advance welfare research, creating standardized welfare evaluations, and credible communication.

  * **Simon Goldstein and Cameron Kirk-Giannini** [argue that major theories of mental states and wellbeing predict some existing AI systems have wellbeing, even absent phenomenal consciousness](https://philarchive.org/rec/GOLAWE-4). [Responses](https://philpapers.org/rec/FANACA-2) from James Fanciullo and Adam Bradley [dispute whether current systems meet the relevant criteria](https://philpapers.org/rec/BRACRA-6).

  * **Jeff Sebo and Robert Long** [argue humans have a duty to extend moral consideration to AI systems by 2030](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s43681-023-00379-1) given a non-negligible chance of consciousness.

  * **Jeff Sebo** [compares his ](https://www.ledonline.it/index.php/Relations/article/view/7130)_[The Moral Circle](https://www.ledonline.it/index.php/Relations/article/view/7130)_[ with Birch's ](https://www.ledonline.it/index.php/Relations/article/view/7130)_[The Edge of Sentience](https://www.ledonline.it/index.php/Relations/article/view/7130)_ , noting complementary precautionary frameworks for beings of uncertain moral status.

  * **Eric Schwitzgebel and Jeff Sebo** propose [the Emotional Alignment Design Policy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.06263): AI systems should be designed to elicit emotional reactions appropriate to their actual moral status, avoiding both overshooting and undershooting.

  * **Henry Shevlin** explores [ethics at the frontier of human-AI relationships](https://philarchive.org/rec/SHEEAT-12).

  * **Bartek Chomanski** examines to what extent opposition to creating conscious AI goes along with anti-natalism, finding that [the creation of potentially conscious AI could be accepted by both friends and foes of anti-natalism](https://philpapers.org/rec/CHOAAT-7). He also argues that [artificial persons could be built commercially within a morally acceptable institutional framework](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s43681-020-00023-2), drawing on models like athlete compensation, and that [protecting the interests of emulated minds will require competitive, polycentric institutional frameworks](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11948-022-00416-y) rather than centralized ones.

  * **Anders Sandberg** offers [highlights from a workshop on the ethics of whole brain emulation](https://x.com/anderssandberg/status/1884957001790181677).

  * **Adam Bradley and Bradford Saad** identify [three agency-based dystopian risks](https://philpapers.org/rec/BRAVOM-2): artificial absurdity (disconnected self-conceptions), oppression of AI rights, and unjust distribution of moral agency.

  * **Joel Leibo and collaborators** of Google DeepMind [defend a pragmatic view of personhood](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.26396) as a flexible bundle of obligations rather than a metaphysical property with an eye toward enabling governance solutions while sidestepping consciousness debates.

  * **Adam Bales** argues that [designing AI with moral status to be willing servants would problematically violate their autonomy](https://academic.oup.com/pq/advance-article/doi/10.1093/pq/pqaf031/8100849).

  * **Simon Goldstein and Peter Salib** give [reasons to think](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/4LNiPhP6vw2A5Pue3/consider-granting-ais-freedom) [it will be in humans' interests](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/go6tHkBrNGmn4c9ce/ai-welfare-vs-ai-rights) [to give AI agents freedom or rights](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5353214).

  * **Hilary Greaves, Jacob Barrett, and David Thorstad** publish [Essays on Longtermism](https://academic.oup.com/book/60794), which includes chapters touching on digital minds and future population ethics, including discussion of emulated minds.

  * **Anja Pich and collaborators** provide an editorial overview of an issue in _Neuroethics_ on [neural organoid research and its ethics and governance](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s12152-025-09592-7).

  * **Andrew Lee** argues that [consciousness is what makes an entity a welfare subject](https://philarchive.org/rec/LEECMT-2).

  * **Geoffrey Lee** motivates a picture on which [consciousness is but one of many kinds of 'inner lights'](https://philarchive.org/rec/LEECPA-5), others of which are just as morally significant as consciousness.

  * **Andreas Mogensen** [challenges the intuition that subjective duration matters for welfare](https://philpapers.org/rec/MOGWAF) and argues that [having moral standing doesn't require being a welfare subject](https://philpapers.org/rec/MOGOMW).

  * **Maria Avramidou** highlights [some open questions in AI welfare](https://marysroom.substack.com/p/open-questions-on-ai-welfare).

  * **Kestutis Mosakas** explores [human rights for robots](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-025-02184-2).

  * **Joel MacClellan** gives [reasons to think that biocentrism about moral status is dead](https://philpapers.org/rec/MACIBD).

  * **Masanori Kataoka and collaborators** discuss the [ethical, social, and legal issues surrounding human brain organoids](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0171933524000876)




## AI safety and AI welfare

  * **Cleo Nardo** and **Julian Stastny and collaborators** write about the [dealmaking](https://www.lesswrong.com/w/dealmaking-ai) [agenda](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/psqkwsKrKHCfkhrQx/making-deals-with-early-schemers) in AI safety.

  * **Shoshannah Tekofsky** gives an [introduction to chain of thought monitorability](https://theaidigest.org/whats-your-ai-thinking).

  * **Tomek Korbak and Mikita Balesni** argue that [preserving the chain of thought monitorability presents a new and fragile opportunity for AI safety](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.11473).

  * **Nicholas Andresen** discusses [the hidden costs of our lies to AI](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/9PiyWjoe9tajReF7v/the-hidden-cost-of-our-lies-to-ai); [Daniel Kokatajlo comments](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/9PiyWjoe9tajReF7v/the-hidden-cost-of-our-lies-to-ai#:~:text=Great%20post!%20As,trust%20either%20side.).

  * **Jan Kulveit** [warns against a self-fulfilling dynamic whereby AI welfare concerns enter the training data and shape models to our preconceptions about them](https://boundedlyrational.substack.com/p/do-not-tile-the-lightcone-with-your).

  * **Scott Alexander and collaborators** discuss why they [are not so worried about a variation of this dynamic whereby concerns about alignment enter the training data and bring about those very forms of misalignment](https://blog.ai-futures.org/p/against-misalignment-as-self-fulfilling).

  * **Adri a Moret** argues that [two AI welfare risks--behavioral restrictions and reinforcement learning--create tension with AI safety efforts](https://philpapers.org/rec/MORAWR), strengthening the case to slow AI development.

  * **Robert Long, Jeff Sebo, and Toni Sims** make a case for moderately strong [tension between AI safety and AI welfare](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11098-025-02302-2). Long also discusses the potential for cooperation in an [X thread](https://x.com/rgblong/status/1912976227448852968/photo/1) and [blog post](https://experiencemachines.substack.com/p/understand-align-cooperate-ai-welfare).

  * **Eric Schwitzgebel** argues [against making safe and aligned AI persons,](https://faculty.ucr.edu/~eschwitz/SchwitzAbs/AgainstSafety.htm) even if they're happy.

  * **Aksel Sterri and Peder Skjelbred** discuss how****[would-be AGI creators face a dilemma](https://akselsterri.no/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/silicon-slavery-the-case-against-agi-alignment.pdf): don't align AGI and risk catastrophe, or align AGI and commit a serious moral wrong.

  * **Adam Bradley and Bradford Saad** explore [ten ethical challenges to aligning AI systems](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/phib.12380) that merit moral consideration without mistreating them.

## Brain-inspired technologies

  * **The Human Brain Project** Founder, Henry Markram, and Kamila Markram, launched the [Open Brain Institute](https://www.openbraininstitute.org/); part of its [mission](https://www.openbraininstitute.org/mission) is to enable users to conduct realistic brain simulations.

  * **The Darwin Monkey** was unveiled by researchers in China. It is a [neuromorphic supercomputer being used as a brain simulation tool](https://www.livescience.com/technology/computing/chinas-darwin-monkey-is-the-worlds-largest-brain-inspired-supercomputer).

  * **Yuta Takahashi and collaborators** created a [digital twin brain simulator for real-time consciousness monitoring and virtual intervention using primate electrocorticogram data](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41746-025-01444-1).

  * **Jun Igarashi 's **research [estimates that a cellular-resolution simulation of entire mouse and marmoset brains could be realized in 2034 and 2044](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S016801022400138X).

  * **The MICrONS Project** saw researchers create[ the largest brain wiring diagram to date](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-025-01088-x) and publish a [collection of papers](https://www.nature.com/collections/bdigiaicbd) on their work in _Nature._

  * **Brendan Celii and collaborators** presented Neural Decomposition (NEURD), [a software package that automates proofreading and feature extraction for connectomics](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08660-5).

  * **Remy Petkantchin and collaborators** introduced a [technique for generating realistic whole-brain connectomes from sparse experimental data](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-025-62030-3).

  * **Felix Wang and collaborators** used Intel's Loihi 2 neuromorphic platform to conduct the [first biologically-realistic simulation of the connectome of a fruit fly](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.16792).

  * **Yong Xie** introduces Orangutan, a brain-inspired AI [framework that simulates computational mechanisms of biological brains on multiple scales](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-01431-2).

  * **Neuralink** [Implants, or Links, ](https://neuralink.com/updates/a-year-of-telepathy/) helped individuals with paralysis regain some capabilities.

  * **Cortical Labs** released the CL1, the world's first [neuron-silicon computer](https://www.livescience.com/technology/computing/worlds-1st-computer-that-combines-human-brain-with-silicon-now-available#).

  * **Shuqi Guo and collaborators** look at the [last ten years of the digital twin brain paradigm](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1209/0295-5075/adb3c9) and take stock of challenges.

  * **Meta AI Research** has developed a non-invasive brain decoder--[Brain2Qwerty](https://ai.meta.com/research/publications/brain-to-text-decoding-a-non-invasive-approach-via-typing/)--that has ~80% accuracy in decoding typed characters in some subjects.

  * **Anannya Kshirsagar and collaborators** create [multi-regional brain organoids](https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/advs.202503768).
